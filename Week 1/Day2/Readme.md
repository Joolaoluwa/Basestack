# Regions, Availabilty Zones and Edge Locations

Regions, availability zones and edge locations
AWS cloud is one of the most used cloud platforms in the world with about 39 geographical regions and 123 availability zones. AWS is favored among its users due to certain reasons such as

- Low-latency: time delay in retrieving information from a data center
- Laws and regulation binding companies: Certain laws prevent businesses from storing data or relevant information outside a geographical region
- redundancy and fault-tolerance: users can design their infrastructure in order to account for failure in service and provide availability for their users during period of high demand
  AWS is divided into Regions, availability zones, edge locations, local zones and wavelength zone.
- Regions are certain geographical zones with two or more availability zones. Regions are isolated from each other
- Availability zones are data centers with independent power, cooling and network infrastructure
- Edge locations serve as points for caching content to serve the end users
- Local zones are an extension of the AWS region, they are located in metropolitan areas
- Wavelength zones are zones with AWS infrastructure included with a telecom network provider for ultra low-latency and 5g network applications
